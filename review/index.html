Due Date

Todd Philips

Overview of story

Due date is a film about an ever flustered business man in a swirl of angst before the birth of his first child. Played by Robert Downey Jr. his character Peter Highman is in an obvious binge of consumption to make sure everything goes according to plan in regards to his wife’s birthing experience. His trademarked anxiety is shown via a barrage of voicemail’s left to his wife about possible names for the baby, how everything will be perfect, he is indeed so in love! Of course the movie is predicated on the fact, that everything that can go wrong will go wrong. This is where we meet the antagonist in the story, Ethan Tremblay. Played by Zac Galifinakis.

The two characters meet as Peter is rushing to the airport to board a flight back to Los Angeles where his wife is having a baby. Upon his arrival, he is shocked by the door of his taxi car being taken out by a speeding vehicle. This of course is the car in which Ethan Tremblay is exiting. The two exchange words, which leads to the mix up of their luggage. As Peter rushes through airport security, he arrives on aboard the plane. And what do you know? Ethan is the seat behind him. The two exchange unpleasant words in which garners attention. Which then leads to their removal off the plane. They are then forced to ride along together in a rental car to Los Angeles, with an onslaught of bumps and road blocks on the way. To say the least! To understand their relationship here is a brief exchange during the initial stages of their journey. “Peter Highman: I despise who you are on a cellular level. Ethan Tremblay: Okay, I've heard that before and I'm trying to work on it”.

The movie approaches the situation with but logic. It is a comedy movie full of laughs predicated on the fact that multiple scenarios in which the two character’s experiences are ridiculous and outrageous. Such as, being kicked off a plane by a U.S Marshall because Ethan said the word bomb. Which got them on a no fly list. Or their heroic escape from Mexico as they accidentally wandered into the U.S-Mexico border because they were high. This scenario lead them to be held captive by Mexican border patrol agents in which Ethan embarks on a mission to break them free by hijacking a law enforcement vehicle from Mexican Authorities. This same vehicle was then used to drive half way across the country. Not realistic, but tremendously hilarious. The adventures that two strangers fall into, filled with laughs and humor. While you watch you sense the hatred toward Ethan coming from Peter. All this remains because Ethan is the cause of this whole mess. It is a constant push and pull between, bickering, fighting and laughing of the two characters.

Despite all the outrageous and unrealistic scenarios seen in the film, you laughter is then curtailed into the larger sentiment of the film. That is, how two strangers meet under circumstances that are less than ideal to then become friends in the end. You're left with a sore stomach from laughter but a sentiment or ethos, although cheesy. Nonetheless, that friendship is a very important thread to our existence.
